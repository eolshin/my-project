Revise a Resume Project Tutorial

Welcome to this Revise a Resume project tutorial! Follow the steps below to customize this resume and make it reflect your qualifications and accomplishments as a webpage builder.

Be sure not to include any personal information not called for by the tutorial. If you're an adult, feel free to add any information you'd like later. If you're not an adult, get help and permission from a parent or guardian deciding what information to share online.

Pay special attention to which information appears inside heading tags like <h1> through <h4>, which information appears inside paragraphs with the <p> tag and which information appears inside unordered lists (<ul>) using the list item tag, <li>.

Follow along as we work through the tutorial together, or else try it on your own. You can also check out this cheatsheet or style guide for ideas of what tags to use if you want to try to work ahead.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Change your name

    Change the name at line 23 of the file called index.html (which you can find in the FILES sidebar on the left) to a name you want to use for this project. If you want to use your own name, for now, don't use anything more than your first name and last initial.

    If that feels like too much information to share, maybe just use your first initial or a name you make up for this project.

    Leave the fake, non-existant address alone for now.

  2. Change your objective

    Think about what you want to achieve with this resume. Is there a particular educational program or opportunity you want to participate in to develp your skills as a webpage builder? Is there a job you want? Or a role - like, "To become the best shoe-blogger in the world?"

    Revise the objective at line 40 of index.html to reflect your goal in composing and sharing this resume.

  3. Change your education

    Beginning at line 47 of index.html, change the educational experiences listed on the resume to match your own experiences in learning how to make a webpage. List the dates and names of any and all learning opportunities that helped you make it this far.

    You do not need to list when or where these educational experiences took place if you feel like that's too much information to share.

  4. Change your experiences

    Next, beginning at line 55 of index.html, list the experiences you've had that have let you apply what you know about building webpages. Maybe you helped update a family member's business page or set up a website for a school publication.

    Remember to include the date, the name of the experience, and a brief description of what you did after the line-break tags - <br> - in each item.

    You do not need to list when or where these webpage building experiences took place if you feel like that's too much information to share.

  5. Change your skills

    Start at line 64 of index.html and list all of the webpage building skills you've gained so far. You might list things like, "Familiar with common HTML tags and webpage structure," or, "Experienced with basic webpage design in HTML."

  6. Change your languages

    If you've worked with any CSS, JavaScript, or other programming platforms before, you can note them at line 71 of index.html by adding new list items.

  7. Style your information

    Review your resume and think through which pieces of information you really want to emphasize for your reader. Add bold (<strong>), italics (<em>), or underline (<u>) tags to highlight essential information on your resume.

Nicely done! You've revised your own webpage-builder resume to share your qualifications and accomplishments online. Feel free to update the resume - or to create a new copy - and use it to pursue educational and career opportunities that interest you. If you're not an adult, be sure to talk with a parent or guardian before adding any more personal information to the page.
